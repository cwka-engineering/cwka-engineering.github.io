# ANSI/HPVA HP-1 Reference

Internal reference for hardwood plywood veneer grading under ANSI/HPVA HP-1-2024. AWI's Architectural Woodwork Standards reference HP-1 directly for veneer grading — understanding HP-1 is required for correct submittal preparation, veneer procurement, and receiving inspection.

> See also: [AWI Standards Reference](/reference/awi-standards.html)


*Source: ANSI/HPVA HP-1-2024 (supersedes HP-1-2020), approved August 20, 2024. For disputed grading calls or formal submittals, reference the full standard directly.*

---

## HP-1 Grade to AWI Grade Mapping

| AWI Grade | HP-1 Face Grade | HP-1 Back Grade | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Premium** | AA | 1 or 2 | All components from same flitch, book matched unless spec says otherwise |
| **Custom** | A | 2 | Slightly wider tolerances on pin knots, component widths, and sapwood allowances |

If a project spec says "AWI Premium, plain-sliced white oak, book matched" — every Grade AA requirement below applies. If it says "AWI Custom" — Grade A applies.

---

## Veneer Matching

### Face Match Types

**Book Match** — Adjacent leaves from the same flitch are opened like a book. Grain mirrors at the splice. Creates a characteristic light/dark alternation when viewed at an angle — this is inherent to book matching and is *not* a defect.

**Slip Match** — Leaves slide across each other without flipping. Grain repeats rather than mirrors. Must be *specified* by the buyer — it is never the default for Grade AA or A.

**Pleasing Match** — Components are assembled for agreeable appearance but need not be grain-matched at joints. Sharp color contrasts at joints are still not permitted. This is the default for **Grade B only**.

### Panel Arrangement Types

| Arrangement | Description | Default? |
|---|---|---|
| **Running Match** | Components run consecutively through the flitch; leftover from one panel starts the next | Manufacturer's option unless otherwise specified |
| **Balanced Match** | Two or more components of equal width make up the face | Must be specified |
| **Center Match** | Even number of equal-width components with a joint on the panel centerline | Must be specified |

> **Important:** Unless the spec explicitly calls out balanced or center match, the manufacturer may use running match at their option. If the architect wants balanced or center match, it must appear in the spec or be established at shop drawing approval — otherwise we are entitled to running match.

### Flitch Continuity

**Grades AA and A:** All components of a book or slip matched face shall be from the same flitch. No mixing flitches within a single panel face.

**Grade B:** Same-flitch requirement applies only if book or slip match is specified. Otherwise, faces must be pleasing matched for color — no sharp color contrasts at joints.

### Sequence Matching Across Panels

HP-1 does not itself require panel-to-panel sequence matching — that is an AWI or spec-driven requirement. Flitch continuity within each panel is what makes sequencing possible: if flitch is broken within a panel, sequencing across panels is meaningless.

When a project spec calls for sequence-matched panels (common on AWI Premium elevation runs):
1. Confirm the flitch yield can cover the full run before committing
2. Track leaf numbering through the nesting and CNC process
3. Coordinate with the veneer supplier on flitch inventory upfront

## Face Component Width Minimums

Minimums below are nominal — outside components may be narrower to accommodate edge trim and matching geometry.

### Grades AA and A

| Cut | Grade AA Min. | Grade A Min. |
|---|---|---|
| Plain-Sliced | 6" [152 mm] | 5" [127 mm] |
| Quarter / Rift / Comb | 3" [76 mm] | 3" [76 mm] |
| Rotary | 6" [152 mm] | 5" [127 mm] |

**Door faces** have tighter minimums: AA door faces drop to 5" [127 mm] plain-sliced and 3" [76 mm] quarter/rift.

### Grade B

| Cut | Grade B Min. |
|---|---|
| Plain-Sliced / Quarter / Rift / Comb | 3" [76 mm] |
| Rotary | 4" [102 mm] |

## Species-Specific Rules

### White Oak and Red Oak

- **Ray fleck in quartered oak:** Slight, blending in AA through B. Unlimited fleck is permitted in quartered faces unless otherwise specified. If the spec says "rift" and the architect wants no visible fleck: fleck ≤ ⅜" [9.5 mm] wide for rift, ≤ ³⁄₃₂" [2.4 mm] for comb grain.
- **Grain slope:** Rift max 1" in 12" [25.4 mm in 305 mm]. Comb grain max ½" in 12" [12.7 mm in 305 mm].
- **Sapwood in White Oak Grade A:** 5% sap allowed in rotary only unless otherwise specified. In rift/comb/quarter/plain-sliced: 10% sap at Grade B level; 20% at rotary.
- **Sapwood in Red Oak Grade AA:** Not allowed.

### Walnut and Cherry

- **Sapwood:** Not allowed in Grade AA. Not allowed in A or B *unless the percentage is agreed upon between buyer and seller*. If a spec says "Grade A walnut" and does not address sapwood, an RFI is required before proceeding.
- **Pin knots:** Conspicuous pin knots (sound knots ≤ ¼" [6.4 mm] with dark centers > ¹⁄₁₆" [1.6 mm]) are restricted by grade. Blending pin knots (dark centers ≤ ¹⁄₁₆" [1.6 mm]) are allowed in all grades.
- **Gum spots in cherry:** Occasional in AA and A. Gum spots *and* gum streaks allowed in Grade B.

### Maple, Birch, Ash, Poplar

- **Color options:** AA and A are available in Sapwood (white), Heartwood (red/brown), or Natural — must be specified.
- **Mineral streaks:** Not allowed in Grade AA (exception: slight for maple). Slight allowed in Grade A.
- **Burls/pin knots:** AA allows up to 2 inconspicuous per 1 m² (approx. 6 per 4×8 panel). A allows 4 per 1 m² (approx. 10 per panel).

### Mahogany, Sapele, Anegre, Makore

- **Sapwood:** Not permitted in AA, A, or B.
- **Cross bars:** "Occasional" in AA and A — more restrictive than oak.
- **Unfilled wormholes:** Not permitted in any grade through B.

## Reconstituted (Recon) Veneer

Recon veneer must be labeled with a descriptor such as "Recon," "Reconstituted," or "Engineered" before the species name (e.g., "Recon Quartered Teak"). This is a **federal law compliance requirement** — recon veneer is typically made from obeche or poplar, not the species it visually mimics.

HP-1 face grade tables (AA through E) do **not** apply to recon veneer. It falls under Specialty Grade (SP); characteristics are agreed between buyer and seller. Do not spec or receive recon veneer as if it were a standard HP-1 grade.

## Core Type Comparison

Ratings: 1 = Poor, 5 = Excellent. (Source: HP-1 Figure 2 / Timber Products Co.)

| Core Type | Screwholding | Machinability | Laminating Surface | Weight | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Veneer Core (3/5/7-ply) | 5 | 4 | 3 | 5 (lightest) | 3 |
| MDF Core | 3 | 5 | 5 | 2 (heaviest) | 4 |
| Particleboard Core | 3 | 3 | 4 | 2 (heavy) | 5 |
| Combination Core (composite crossbands + VC) | 4 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 4 |
| OSB Core | 3 | 3 | 4 | 2 (heavy) | 5 |

For AWI Premium/Custom casework, **MDF core or combination core** are most common due to superior laminating surface and machinability. **Veneer core** is preferred where weight or screwholding is critical (shelving, structural applications).

> Note: AWI prohibits veneer core for cabinet door and drawer front components regardless of HP-1 grade. See [AWI Standards Reference — Panel Product Rules](/reference/awi-standards.html#panel-product-rules).

## Dimensional Tolerances

Thickness is measured at mid-width of one end. If out of tolerance, three additional measurements are taken and the average of all four governs.

| Dimension | Tolerance |
|---|---|
| Width | ± ¹⁄₃₂" [± 0.8 mm] |
| Length | ± ¹⁄₃₂" [± 0.8 mm] |
| Thickness ≥ ¼" [6.4 mm] | − ³⁄₆₄" [1.2 mm] from stated class |
| Thickness < ¼" [6.4 mm] | − ¹⁄₃₂" [0.8 mm] from stated class |
| Squareness, panels > 4×4 ft | Within ³⁄₃₂" [2.4 mm] diagonal difference |
| Squareness, panels ≤ 4×4 ft | Within ¹⁄₁₆" [1.6 mm] diagonal difference |
| Straightness, panels ≤ 8 ft | Within ¹⁄₁₆" [1.6 mm] |
| Straightness, panels > 8 ft | Within ³⁄₃₂" [2.4 mm] |

## Bond Line Types

| Type | Bond | Standard Use |
|---|---|---|
| Technical / Type I | Fully waterproof (exterior) | High-moisture areas; not for continuous submersion |
| **Type II** | **Water resistant (interior)** | **Standard for interior casework and millwork** |

Type II is the default for our work. Type I is required only when the spec calls it out for wet areas (labs, commercial kitchens, etc.).

## Formaldehyde Compliance

All hardwood plywood must comply with CARB ATCM, EPA TSCA Title VI, and Canada's CANFER. Emission limit for hardwood plywood: **0.05 ppm** (ASTM E1333 large-scale chamber or ASTM D6007 small-chamber equivalent).

NAF (No Added Formaldehyde) and ULEF (Ultra Low Emitting Formaldehyde) resins may qualify for exempt status at 0.04–0.05 ppm. **Labeling is mandatory** per TSCA on all panels or bundles — verify on incoming material.

## Back Grades

| Characteristic | Grade 1 | Grade 2 | Grade 3 | Grade 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sound tight knots max dia. | ⅜" [9.5 mm] | ¾" [19 mm] | 1½" [38 mm] | Unlimited |
| Knotholes | 6 max, repaired to ⅛" | Repaired to ½" | 1" [25.4 mm] (avail. repaired) | 4" [102 mm] |
| Splits/open joints | Repaired, ⅛" × 12" max | Repaired, ³⁄₁₆" × 12" max | ⅜" × ¼ panel length | Up to 1" wide |
| Rough cut / ruptured grain | Two 8" areas | 5% of panel | Unlimited | Unlimited |

For AWI Premium, specify **Grade 1 back** where the back is exposed (open shelving, glass-front cabinets). Grade 2 is typical for concealed backs.

---

## Moisture Content

Panels shall not exceed **12% MC** at time of shipment from the mill (oven-dry test per §4.7). Test specimens must be ≥ 9 sq. in. [5,806 mm²] and ≥ 20 grams.

---

## Product Marking and Shipment Tolerance

Every industrial panel ≥ ½" [12.7 mm] thick must be individually stamped with: standard designation (ANSI/HPVA HP-1-2024), producer ID, face species, face grade, and back grade if a different species. Panels under ½" may be marked per unit or pallet.

**5% shipment tolerance:** Up to 5% of a shipment may be non-conforming before the entire shipment is rejectable under re-inspection. Non-conforming panels should be flagged at receiving and held — do not incorporate into work before disposition.

## Definitions

**Blending** — A color change detectable at 6–8 ft that does not detract from overall appearance. This is the threshold for "inconspicuous" characteristics in the grade tables.

**Sharp Contrasts** — Lighter-than-average veneer joined to darker-than-average veneer, or widely dissimilar grain or figure between adjacent components. Prohibited in Grades AA, A, and B — including in pleasing match.

**Split Heart** — Creating a cathedral figure by joining two components. Not allowed in Grade AA (cathedral must come from a single component). Allowed in Grade A through E subject to minimum component width rules.

**Rough Cut / Ruptured Grain** — Not allowed in AA or A. "Slight" in B. QC inspection point on incoming panels.

**Knife Mark** — A linear indentation or raised strip deep or high enough to be felt with a fingernail. A defect under smooth, tight-cut requirements for AA and A.

**Shop** — A panel with defects outside any HP-1 grade, or one not designated to a grade. Not acceptable for AWI Premium or Custom unless explicitly accepted in writing.
